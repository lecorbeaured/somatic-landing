Frequently Asked Questions

What is The Somatic Anxiety Reset?
The Somatic Anxiety Reset is a 30-day nervous system workbook for women. It contains one body-based somatic practice per day, ten minutes at a time, sequenced across four weeks: Ground, Release, Regulate, and Restore. It is built on somatic experiencing, polyvagal theory, and trauma-informed clinical practice.
What is somatic therapy for anxiety?
Somatic therapy for anxiety is a body-based approach that works directly with the nervous system rather than thoughts or stories about anxiety. It uses practices like vagus nerve activation, bilateral stimulation, breathwork, and physical release techniques to complete the stress cycle and restore the nervous system to a regulated baseline.
How long does each practice take?
Every practice in The Somatic Anxiety Reset takes ten minutes or less. The workbook is designed for real life -- no yoga mat, no quiet retreat, and no perfect morning required.
Do I need experience with somatic work?
No. The Somatic Anxiety Reset starts from the very beginning and builds progressively. No prior experience with somatic work, yoga, or breathwork is required. The only requirement is a willingness to show up for ten minutes a day.
What formats is the book available in?
The Somatic Anxiety Reset is available as a paperback ($17.99) on Amazon, a Kindle eBook ($8.99) on Amazon, and an instant PDF download ($26) on Payhip. The PDF can be printed at home or used digitally on iPad with GoodNotes or Notability.
Does this replace therapy?
No. The Somatic Anxiety Reset is somatic education, not therapy. It is designed to complement professional mental health care -- as between-session practice, as a self-guided resource, or for women who have completed therapy and want to continue deepening their regulation practice independently.
What is polyvagal theory?
Polyvagal theory, developed by Dr. Stephen Porges, describes how the vagus nerve regulates the nervous system's response to safety and threat. In anxiety, the nervous system gets stuck in a state of chronic activation. Polyvagal-informed somatic practices like those in this workbook directly stimulate the vagus nerve to shift the nervous system toward calm.
